# ADR-NNNN: Short present-tense title

- **Status:** Proposed | Accepted | Deprecated | Superseded by ADR-NNNN
- **Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
- **Deciders:** who agreed to this

## Context

What situation forces a decision? Constraints, requirements, and what happens if nothing is decided. Facts and pressures only — no solution yet.

## Decision

State what you decided, actively and unambiguously: "We will ...".

## Alternatives considered

### Option A — the one chosen

Why it wins.

### Option B

Why you rejected it. Be fair to it. An alternative that sounds obviously bad was never a real alternative, and the reader then doubts the analysis.

### Option C

Same.

## Consequences

**Good**

- What becomes easier or cheaper

**Bad**

- What becomes harder or more expensive. Every real decision has entries here. If this section is empty, the analysis is incomplete.

**Neutral**

- What changes without clearly being better or worse

## Revisit when

The conditions that would make this decision wrong: a scale threshold, a pricing change, a dependency going unmaintained. This is what turns an ADR from a historical note into a warning.
